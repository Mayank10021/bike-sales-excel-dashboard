## 📌 Project Overview

An end-to-end Excel project that transforms raw bike buyers data into an interactive, insight-driven sales dashboard. This project demonstrates a complete data analytics workflow — from raw data cleaning to a polished, filterable dashboard — entirely within Microsoft Excel.

---

## 🎯 Objective

To analyze bike purchasing behavior across customer demographics (income, gender, age, commute distance) and present findings through a dynamic Excel dashboard with slicers for real-time filtering.

## 📊 Dashboard Components

### 1. Average Income per Purchase (by Gender)
- Compares average income of customers who did vs. did not purchase a bike, split by gender
- Insight: Higher-income individuals across both genders showed higher purchase rates

### 2. Customer Commute Distance Analysis
- Displays bike purchase frequency across commute distance ranges (0–1 mi, 1–2 mi, 5–10 mi, 10+ mi)
- Insight: Short commute customers show the highest purchase tendency

### 3. Customer Age Bracket Analysis
- Groups customers into Adolescent, Middle-Aged, and Old categories
- Insight: Middle-aged customers are the dominant buyer segment

### 🎛️ Interactive Filters (Slicers)
- Marital Status
- Region
- Education Level

## 📋 Dataset Details

- **Source:** [Kaggle – Bike Buyers Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/heeraldedhia/bike-buyers)
- **Size:** 1,000 rows × 13 columns
- **Key Columns:** ID, Marital Status, Gender, Income, Children, Education, Occupation, Home Owner, Cars, Commute Distance, Region, Age, Purchased Bike

---

## 🚀 How to Use

1. Clone or download this repository
2. Open `Bike Sales - Excel Dashboard Project.xlsx` in Microsoft Excel (2016 or later recommended)
3. Navigate to the **Dashboard** sheet
4. Use the slicers on the left to filter by Marital Status, Region, and Education
5. Observe how all three charts update dynamically

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Middle-aged customers (31–54)** have the highest bike purchase rate across all regions
- **Customers with short commutes (0–1 mile)** are the most likely bike buyers
- **Males with higher income** are slightly more likely to purchase than females in the same income range
- **North America** shows the highest overall sales volume in the dataset
